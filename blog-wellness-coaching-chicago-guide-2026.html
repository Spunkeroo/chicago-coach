What Is Wellness Coaching

Wellness coaching is a client-centered process that empowers individuals to make sustainable health behavior changes. Unlike personal trainers who tell you what to do, or nutritionists who prescribe diets, wellness coaches help you discover YOUR path to better health.

The National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching (NBHWC) defines health coaching as partnering with clients to identify health goals, develop strategies, and create accountability for lasting lifestyle changes.

Types of Wellness Coaching Available in Chicago

  • Integrative Health Coaching: Combines nutrition, exercise, stress management, and sleep optimization into a holistic plan
  • Nutrition Coaching: Focuses on dietary habits, meal planning, and relationship with food
  • Fitness Coaching: Exercise programming, movement habits, and physical performance
  • Stress and Mindfulness Coaching: Meditation, breathwork, stress reduction techniques
  • Chronic Disease Management: Support for diabetes, heart disease, autoimmune conditions
  • Corporate Wellness: Workplace wellness programs and employee coaching

Credentials to Look For

The most important credential is NBC-HWC (National Board Certified Health and Wellness Coach). This requires: a coaching-specific education from an NBHWC-approved program, 50+ hours of supervised coaching practice, passing a rigorous national exam, and ongoing continuing education.

Other valuable credentials include ACE Health Coach, ACSM Certified Exercise Physiologist, and IIN Health Coach. In Chicago, Northwestern Medicine, Rush University, and UIC all have affiliated wellness coaching programs.

What to Expect From Wellness Coaching Sessions

A typical wellness coaching engagement includes:

  1. Initial assessment (60-90 minutes): Health history, current habits, goals, readiness for change
  2. Regular sessions (30-60 minutes, weekly or biweekly): Goal setting, obstacle identification, strategy development
  3. Between-session support: Accountability check-ins, progress tracking, resource sharing
  4. Program length: Most programs run 3-6 months for lasting behavior change

Benefits Backed by Research

Peer-reviewed research supports wellness coaching effectiveness:

  • Weight management: Coached participants lost 2x more weight than self-directed individuals (Journal of Medical Internet Research)
  • Diabetes management: A1C levels improved by 0.5-1.0% with coaching support (Diabetes Care)
  • Stress reduction: 68% of coached participants reported significant stress reduction (American Journal of Lifestyle Medicine)
  • Exercise adherence: Coached individuals were 3x more likely to maintain exercise habits at 12 months
