What Executive Coaching Covers

Leadership Presence

How you show up in meetings, with your board, and with your team. Executive presence combines communication, confidence, gravitas, and emotional intelligence. This is the #1 reason Chicago executives seek coaching.

Strategic Thinking

Moving from operational management to strategic leadership. Learning to think in systems, anticipate market shifts, and make decisions with incomplete information.

Stakeholder Management

Navigating board relationships, investor communications, cross-functional leadership, and organizational politics. Critical for anyone in Chicago's corporate environment.

Work-Life Integration

Executive burnout is at all-time highs. Coaching helps leaders establish sustainable performance practices, set boundaries, and model healthy leadership for their organizations.

How to Choose an Executive Coach in Chicago

  1. Check credentials: PCC or MCC from ICF is the minimum. Look for additional training from programs like CTI, Georgetown, Columbia, or Hudson Institute
  2. Industry experience: The best executive coaches have held leadership roles themselves. Look for former C-suite leaders or senior consultants who pivoted to coaching
  3. Chemistry session: Every reputable coach offers a complimentary chemistry session. Use it to assess fit, ask about their methodology, and discuss your goals
  4. References: Ask for 2-3 client references at your level. A coach who works with Fortune 500 CEOs may not be the right fit for a startup VP
  5. Assessment tools: Top coaches use validated assessments (Hogan, 360-degree feedback, EQ-i, StrengthsFinder) as baselines for development

FAQ

How long does executive coaching take?

Typical engagements run 6-12 months with bi-weekly sessions (90 minutes each). Short-term transition coaching may last 3-4 months. Ongoing maintenance coaching continues indefinitely with monthly check-ins.

Is executive coaching confidential?

Yes. Professional coaches follow strict confidentiality guidelines. In employer-sponsored coaching, the coach reports on themes and progress (with your knowledge) but never shares specific conversation content.

Can I expense executive coaching?

In most cases, yes. Executive coaching is a tax-deductible business expense. If employer-funded, it's typically covered under professional development. Ask your HR or finance team about your company's coaching budget.

What's the difference between executive coaching and consulting?

Consultants give you answers and solutions. Coaches help you find your own answers through powerful questioning, reflection, and accountability. Coaching creates lasting behavior change; consulting provides temporary expertise.
