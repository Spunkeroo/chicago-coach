What Leadership Coaching Covers

  • Executive presence: How you carry yourself in meetings, on stage, and in one-on-ones. Body language, vocal tone, and the ability to command attention without dominating it
  • Strategic thinking: Moving from tactical day-to-day management to big-picture strategy. Learning to set vision and inspire teams toward long-term goals
  • Difficult conversations: Giving constructive feedback, managing underperformers, navigating office politics, and handling conflict with peers and superiors
  • Delegation and empowerment: Many new leaders struggle to delegate effectively. Coaching teaches how to develop team members while maintaining accountability
  • Emotional intelligence: Self-awareness, empathy, and social skill development. Research consistently shows EQ is a stronger predictor of leadership success than IQ
  • Work-life integration: Preventing burnout while performing at a high level. Setting boundaries, managing energy, and sustaining peak performance long-term

Choosing a Leadership Coach

  1. Industry experience: A coach who has led teams in your industry understands the specific challenges you face. Ask about their professional background before coaching
  2. Credential level matters: For executive coaching, seek PCC or MCC level ICF certification. ACC coaches may lack the experience for C-suite complexity
  3. Assessment tools: The best leadership coaches use validated assessments (360-degree feedback, Hogan, DiSC, StrengthsFinder) to establish baselines and measure progress
  4. Chemistry session: Request a paid trial session before committing to a long engagement. Leadership coaching requires deep trust and candor
  5. Measure outcomes: Set specific, measurable goals at the start. Examples: "Receive 4+ rating on next 360 review" or "Promote 2 direct reports within 12 months"

Frequently Asked Questions

Is leadership coaching worth the investment?

The International Coaching Federation reports an average ROI of 788% for leadership coaching. Companies see improvements in productivity, employee satisfaction, retention, and revenue. For individual executives, coaching accelerates promotions and salary growth.

How much does executive coaching cost in Chicago?

Executive coaching in Chicago ranges from $300-$1,000 per session depending on the coach level and client seniority. Typical engagements are 6-12 months with bi-weekly sessions. Many engagements are employer-funded.

What is the difference between leadership coaching and management training?

Management training teaches specific skills in a group setting. Leadership coaching is individualized, addresses your specific challenges, and focuses on behavioral change over time. Coaching produces deeper and more lasting results.

How long does a leadership coaching engagement last?

Most engagements run 6-12 months with sessions every 2 weeks. The first 2-3 months focus on assessment and awareness. Months 3-6 focus on behavior change. Months 6-12 sustain changes and address advanced goals.

Can I get my company to pay for coaching?

Many companies fund coaching for managers and above. Present the ROI data (788% average return) and frame it as professional development. Ask HR about existing coaching budgets or professional development stipends.
